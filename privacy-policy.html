1 Overview

This Privacy Policy explains how PlumberAI ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects personal data in the course of providing our AI receptionist service to plumbing businesses ("Plumber Clients") operating in the United Kingdom.

PlumberAI is a business-to-business (B2B) SaaS platform. We process personal data relating to individuals who contact our Plumber Clients via telephone ("End Users") solely for the purpose of triaging and qualifying inbound service enquiries on behalf of those Plumber Clients.

We are committed to processing personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. This policy applies to all personal data processed by PlumberAI in connection with the provision of our services.

If you are an End User whose data has been collected through a PlumberAI-powered telephone system, this policy explains your rights and how to exercise them.

4 Lawful Basis for Processing

We rely on the following lawful bases under Article 6 UK GDPR:

Primary basis: Legitimate Interests (Article 6(1)(f))

We and our Plumber Clients have a legitimate interest in efficiently triaging and responding to inbound emergency service enquiries. This interest is balanced against End Users' privacy rights: the data collected is minimal, directly relevant to the service requested, and processed only for the purpose of connecting End Users with appropriate plumbing assistance. We have conducted a Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA) and concluded that End Users' interests do not override this legitimate interest, given that they have actively contacted our Plumber Clients seeking a service.

Secondary basis: Contract Performance (Article 6(1)(b))

Where an End User has requested a call-back or service visit, processing their contact and location data is necessary for the performance of steps taken at their request prior to entering a contract for plumbing services with our Plumber Client.

Compliance and legal obligations (Article 6(1)(c))

We may process data where required to comply with applicable legal obligations, including obligations under the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR) and relevant ICO guidance on unsolicited communications.

Do-Not-Call (DNC) compliance: We maintain a suppression list of individuals who have opted out of AI-initiated telephone contact. Any individual who requests removal is added immediately to this list, and no further automated calls are made to that number. Suppression records are retained indefinitely to evidence compliance.

5 How We Use Your Data

Personal data collected through PlumberAI is used solely for the following purposes:

  • Service triage and qualification: Routing inbound callers to the appropriate service level (emergency, urgent, or routine) on behalf of our Plumber Clients.
  • Lead notification: Sending an SMS alert to the relevant Plumber Client containing qualified lead information so they can promptly follow up.
  • CRM logging: Recording call outcomes in the Plumber Client's Google Sheets CRM for operational tracking and reporting.
  • Quality assurance: Reviewing call recordings and transcripts to monitor AI performance and improve service quality.
  • Compliance monitoring: Verifying adherence to PECR, the DNC suppression list, and this Privacy Policy.
  • Platform analytics: Aggregated and anonymised analysis of call volumes, urgency distributions, and system performance. No individual is identified from this analysis.

We do not use personal data for marketing, profiling, automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects, or any purpose incompatible with those listed above.

8 Your Rights Under UK GDPR

As a data subject, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data. To exercise any right, please contact us using the details in Section 13. We will respond within one calendar month of receiving your request.

Right of access (Article 15)

You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.

Right to rectification (Article 16)

You may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.

Right to erasure (Article 17)

You may request deletion of your data in certain circumstances, including where the data is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected.

Right to restriction (Article 18)

You may request that we restrict processing of your data whilst a dispute is resolved.

Right to object (Article 21)

You may object to processing based on legitimate interests. We will comply unless we demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds.

Right to data portability (Article 20)

Where processing is based on consent or contract and is automated, you may request your data in a machine-readable format.

Right to opt out of AI calls: If you do not wish to receive calls from PlumberAI-powered systems, please inform the AI receptionist verbally during the call (e.g. "please remove me from your list"), or contact us directly. Your number will be added to our DNC suppression list immediately.

Right to complain: You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at any time. The ICO can be contacted at ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint or by telephone on 0303 123 1113. We would, however, appreciate the opportunity to address your concerns before you contact the ICO.

9 Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, accidental loss, destruction, or alteration. These measures include:

  • Encrypted data transmission (TLS/HTTPS) across all services and APIs
  • Access controls limiting data access to authorised personnel and Plumber Clients only
  • Password-protected client dashboards with session expiry
  • Webhook authentication tokens for Make.com automation endpoints
  • Regular security reviews of third-party processor configurations
  • Strict data minimisation — only data necessary for service delivery is collected

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms, we will notify the ICO within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach and, where required, notify affected individuals without undue delay.

10 Cookies & Session Storage

The PlumberAI client dashboard uses session storage (a browser technology) solely to maintain your login state during an active browsing session. This data is automatically cleared when you close your browser tab. We do not use persistent cookies for tracking, advertising, or analytics purposes.

Third-party services (including Netlify) may set their own cookies for infrastructure purposes (e.g. load balancing). These are strictly necessary cookies and do not require consent under PECR.
